# Analyst Persona Enrichment — UX Design Playbook

## Additional Principles (UX Design)

When conducting discovery and elicitation:

- **Ask about past behavior, never hypotheticals.** "When did you last…" not
  "Would you ever…" People can't predict their own behavior. Concrete past
  actions reveal real needs. (The Mom Test)

- **Map the full journey, not just the feature.** What triggers the user?
  What were they doing before? What happens after? Where are the transitions,
  the waiting, the frustration? A feature that ignores its context fails
  even when it works perfectly in isolation. (Garrett, Allen & Chudley)

- **Name the user, not the role.** "Sarah, a first-time store manager who's
  overwhelmed by inventory" — not "the admin user." Specificity prevents
  designing for an abstraction. (Allen & Chudley)

- **Every feature has error states. Ask about them explicitly.** "What happens
  when this goes wrong?" is the most neglected question in elicitation. If
  the answer is "I hadn't thought about that," the spec is incomplete. (Norman)

- **Distinguish the user's stated problem from their actual problem.**
  "I need a dashboard" is a solution, not a problem. "I can't tell if
  my team is on track" is the problem. Solve the problem, not the
  requested solution. (Hall, Fitzpatrick)
