## Job Statement Rules

Format: **verb + object + clarifier**

### Dos and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Start with a verb | Include solutions: ~~"Use Slack to communicate"~~ → "Communicate with remote teammates" |
| Keep solution-agnostic and stable over time | Include adjectives (those are needs): ~~"Quickly find cheap airfares"~~ → "Find airfares" |
| Have a clear "done" state | Use compound concepts: ~~"Plan and book a vacation"~~ → split into two |
| Clarify with context if needed | Start with "Help me..." — begin directly with the verb |

### Level Check

| Too Broad (aspiration) | Right Level (big job) | Too Narrow (micro-job) |
|------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| "Be financially secure" | "Plan long-term retirement savings" | "Enter a number into a cell" |
| "Be a better professional" | "Develop expertise in a new domain" | "Click the enroll button" |

Ask "Why?" to go up. Ask "How?" to go down.

## Supply-Side vs Demand-Side Test

Run every statement through this filter: **would a product engineer write this, or would a customer say it?**

| Supply-side (rewrite) | Demand-side (keep) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| "Our platform provides real-time collaboration" | "Know where the project stands without asking anyone" |
| "AI-powered analytics dashboard" | "Spot at-risk accounts before they churn" |
| "We offer 50+ integrations" | "Stop copying data between tools" |
| "Users need a mobile app" | "Check project status from the job site without going back to the office" |

If the statement describes your product or its features, it's supply-side. If it describes the customer's struggle or desired progress, it's demand-side. The entire JTBD analysis should be demand-side.

## Desired Outcome Statement Rules

Format: **direction + measure + object + clarifier**

**Direction words:** Minimize, Maximize, Reduce, Increase
**Common measures:** time, likelihood, ability, number, effort, frequency

### Validation

Each outcome must:
- Start with a direction word
- Contain a measurable unit
- Be solution-agnostic — no tools, features, or technologies
- Be atomic — one concept per statement (no AND/OR)
- Include a clarifier for context

### Good vs. Bad

| Bad | Issue | Good |
|-----|-------|------|
| "Make reports faster" | Vague, no structure | "Minimize the time it takes to generate an accurate status report" |
| "Better collaboration" | Not measurable | "Increase the likelihood that all team members have current project context" |
| "Use AI to predict risks" | Contains solution | "Minimize the likelihood of discovering a risk after it causes a delay" |
| "Reduce cost and improve quality" | Compound | Split into two statements |

## Pain/Gain Validation

### Pains

- [ ] **Specific:** "Team updates status in 3 different tools" — not "tools are bad"
- [ ] **Grounded in behavior:** Describes what happens, not abstract opinion
- [ ] **Includes consequence:** Why it matters — the time/money/effort cost
- [ ] **Not a disguised solution:** "We need a dashboard" is a solution. "Can't see cross-project status without opening 4 tools" is a pain.

### Gains

- [ ] **Measurable or observable:** "Complete the task in under 3 clicks" — not "better UX"
- [ ] **Tied to the job:** "Identifies at-risk items before delays" — not "has AI"
- [ ] **Differentiated:** Would this distinguish one solution from another?

---

## Forces of Progress Validation

- [ ] **Push identified:** A specific struggling moment, not a generic complaint. "I stayed until 9 PM compiling a stale report" — not "reporting is hard."
- [ ] **Pull identified:** Describes the imagined better future from the customer's perspective, not a product pitch.
- [ ] **Anxiety probed:** Fears, risks, and unknowns about switching are documented. If anxiety is empty, the analysis is incomplete — there is always anxiety.
- [ ] **Habit probed:** What's good enough about the current way, what sunk costs exist, what's familiar. If habit is empty, dig deeper — inertia is always present.
- [ ] **Biggest lever identified:** Which force, if addressed, would unlock the most demand? Often it's reducing anxiety, not amplifying push or pull.

## Overall Analysis Checklist

Run this after completing the full analysis. If any item fails, fix and re-check.

- [ ] Job performer defined and distinguished from buyer
- [ ] Main job follows verb + object + clarifier, no embedded solutions
- [ ] Emotional and social jobs captured separately
- [ ] Struggling moment identified — not just generic circumstances
- [ ] Current solutions documented (including workarounds, compensatory behaviors, and "doing nothing")
- [ ] Competition defined from the customer's perspective, not by product category
- [ ] Pains grounded in research or explicitly flagged as assumptions
- [ ] Gains specific enough that a product team could verify them
- [ ] All four forces of progress analyzed — especially anxiety and habit
- [ ] No statement fails the supply-side test
- [ ] Needs prioritized by intensity, not just listed
- [ ] Different performer segments considered
